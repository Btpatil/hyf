    What United Us?

    Failed Bureaucracy

    Former US President Mr. Obama in his book wrote about India's bureaucracy as an impediment in its progress: “Despite its genuine economic progress, though, India remained a chaotic and impoverished place: largely divided by religion and caste, captive to the whims of corrupt local officials and power brokers, hamstrung by a parochial bureaucracy that was resistant to change.” As a country, we don't want anyone's feedback on our bureaucracy and governance but the fact is that despite after 75 years of Independence:

    1. 1. Our bureaucrats remain corrupt, for instance, in Municipalities or any government office, until and unless you don't pay under the table, you will not be allotted a tender or your work will not be done.
    2. 2. The RFPs/RFQs and EOIs that is being floated are generally designed by the influential people, organizations and institutions that creates an entry barrier for smaller but skilled organizations and teams.
    3. 3. There is a huge gap between the skills and understanding of latest technologies, subjects etc. among these bureaucrats. India needs a robust bureaucratic and governance mechanism so that policies can be implemented in a sound-manner on-ground. HYF founding members faced immense issues while dealing with the low and mid-level employees in the government due to their laid-back approach and minimal understanding of various challenges that the entire CSR, ESG, or other industries are facing.

    Corrupt Ecosystem

    Not just the Government offices or bureaucrats but in-general corruption has become a major issue in almost all the facets of life and businesses, for instance, professionals working in the impact industry, certification and compliances industry, etc. are the role models for the future generations but what if they start taking bribe to provide you certifications without even implementing the best practices. Whom would you trust then? An organization? Its processes? Or a piece of paper in a form of certificate?

    The trading mindset that India have given this corrupt ecosystem higher wings, no one wants to put that extra efforts to realize value and potential from a product (or a waste material/resource), but everyone is busy trading it for minimal but easy margins/commissions, etc. They are the masters of bill trading, and compliments this corrupt system amazingly.

    We at HYF if won’t be able to fight with them, at least we will safeguard ourselves and will not compromise our ethics for business, as for us it is not business but a sustainable and scalable impact.

    Alarming ESG Issues

    This is something that needs no explanation, but, more than the issues we should be focussing on identifying right solutions and skills to solve it. The problems that we see in matters related to ESG are:

    1. 1. Most of the investors/businesses are focussing on seeing sustainability from a macro-lens, i.e., carbon emissions, water footprints, packaging related issues, social benefits for the workers, etc. A very crucial “G” i.e., Governance is something that is being left behind among all the “E” Environmental and “S” Social Issues.
    2. 2. Quite often professionals are confused among corporate social responsibility i.e., CSR, and ESG and or Circular Economy initiatives, and they club them together. CSR and Sustainability should be seen very differently as sustainable initiatives are something that should be taken across the value-chain in the operations (be it downstream, mid-stream or upstream).
    3. 3. The skills required to solve such complex issues are still developing and lot of young people are entering into this side of the business, but again the level of understanding and maturity that is required to solve these issues, is still not there.
    4. 4. Most of the senior-management people comes from either communications or PR/Marketing background thus, making it a greenwashing and branding exercise rather than the actual impact.

    HYF was born because of such issues and we believe we would be able to solve each one of them in the projects we do and the collaborations/partnerships we make in the future.
